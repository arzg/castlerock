Castle Rock, Cape Schanck

Description of our podcast

Eugene Von Guérard’s Castle Rock, Cape Schanck is a painting of a large, coastal rock face covered in grass, with a small lighthouse visible on top. This rock face slopes from a steep peak down to sea level. It is at this height at which the ocean is lapping at the deep red rocks. Just at the edge of where the grass has stopped and the rock begins at the sea-level side of the rock face stands a man, holding a long stick and wearing a wide-brimmed hat. This podcast discusses the importance of this painting and why losing it would be devastating.

World

Eugene Von Guérard finds Australia’s landscape very aesthetically pleasing because he appreciates the unique topography.He painted Australia because he came for the gold rush bu then noticed the Australian coastline whilst on a boat migrating to Australia. He was amazed by the natural beauty that was unlike his home. He produced his paintings during the romantic era which focused on emotions, the individual and an increased appreciation of nature. Despite painting in the Romantic period, he painted with a realistic style which focused on reproducing the subject matter as accurately as possible.

Artist

Eugene von Guérard was born in Vienna on 7th of November 1811 with an Austrian and German heritage. At age 15, he travelled along with his father to Italy where he studied old masters and knowledge of painting from his father who worked at the court painter. He later moved to Rome where he studied at Giovanni Patista’s art school after his father passed away. He later migrated to Australia in 1852 for the gold rush. Unfortunately, he failed at finding any gold and decided to paint instead. Artwork

It’s painting expresses many different emotions while most are happy. It also represents joy and hope in this new land.
